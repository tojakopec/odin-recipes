Ingredients

Sofrito

  • 2 yellow onions
  • 3 stalks celery
  • 2 medium to large carrots

Bolognese (Meat Sauce)

  • 750g beef mince
  • 250g pork mince
  • 200ml red or white wine
  • 2x 400g canned peeled plum tomatoes
  • 2x beef stock cubes
  • 2 Tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 bay leaves
  • salt and pepper to taste

Béchamel Sauce

  • 1 litre whole milk
  • 100g plain flour
  • 100g butter
  • freshly grated nutmeg
  • salt and pepper to taste

Other Ingredients (for assembly)

  • grated parmigiano regianno (a lot)
  • cubed or grated mozzarella cheese
  • lasagne sheets

Steps

Sofrito

  1. In a large pot heat up a few tablespoons of olive oil
  2. Once oil is heat, add all the vegetables and coat in oil
  3. Add a few pinches of salt and stir often
  4. When the kitchen is filled with a heavenly scent and your vegetables have shrunk in size, move on to next step...

Bolognese (Meat Sauce)

  1. Add beef and pork mince to your sofrito and use fork to break it up and prevent from clumping
  2. Once completely brown, add wine and bay leaves and cook until wine evaporates
  3. Add tomato paste, tomato cans and stock cubes
  4. Use water to wash out any remaining tomato from cans and add that water to the pot
  5. Once sauce starts to boil, cover, reduce heat and simmer for approximately 90mins
  6. Stir every 5-10 minutes, preventing the sauce from burning and also checking for desired consistency (the sauce should be thick and juicy, not runny and liquid)

Béchamel Sauce

  1. In a pot, add the butter and melt it over medium heat
  2. Gradually add flour, constantly whisking to prevent any clumping
  3. After combining the flour and butter, gradually add warm milk and stir vigorously
  4. Once all the ingredients have been combined into a smooth and creamy sauce, add salt, grated nutmeg and pepper to taste

Assembly

  1. In a large baking dish, start by putting a ladle of the meat sauce on the bottom of the dish, spreading it around evenly
  2. Follow with a layer of lasagne noodles, covering the whole dish and slightly overlapping the noodles
  3. Add a layer of meat sauce and spread it around evenly
  4. Add a layer of the béchamel (partially covering the previous layer in a random pattern)
  5. Sprinkle the mozzarella and parmigiano on top of the sauces
  6. Repeat the previous 4 steps 1-2 times, depending on the size of your dish and the amount of sauces you have (you should leave extra béchamel for the top layer)
  7. On your final layer of noodles, spread a smaller amount of meat sauce and extra béchamel sauce, top with a large amount of grated cheese
  8. Bake in the oven at 180 degrees Celsius for approximately 35 minutes
